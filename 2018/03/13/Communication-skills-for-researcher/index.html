Communication skills for researcher

发表于 2018-03-13 | 更新于: 2018-03-13 | 分类于 Graduate Study | 阅读次数:
字数统计: 983

Here are some suggestions of communication skills for graduate student.

Check your E-mail frequently and respond to the E-mail as soon as possible.

Even if you are not ready to comment on the sender email, you had better respond to the sender by indicating that you have received this email and you will take a while to study the email and give the sender your comment.

Put the original E-mail others send you in the end of your replying E-mail and sign your email.

It is better to write your responding email at the beginning. This is called “top down” culture of communication.

Think of the function of Carbon copy (cc, 抄送) when you are sending an E-mail.

This is also called “putting someone in the loop”. This can make your email more efficiently, as there may be a need to have more people be informed of the information and knowledge in your email message.

Write a Minutes (会议记录) after some important discussion – in particular with your supervisor.

Making presentation (seminar) will benefit your communication.

The goal of your presentation should be to let audiences know you but not show how smart you are. Even though you are indeed very smart, if you fail to deliver your message to audiences, your smart behavior is Nil.

Writing report or giving a talk should give sufficient background.

But this should not compromise the top-down communication.

When you communicate with others, you need to put your thesis sentence up to the front or your standpoint up to the front. For instance, you may start with: “I do not completely agree with you. Let me first tell you some background. …., now let me explain where I feel I would like to discuss with you further.”

Do not do in the other way around: first tell your audience background, and then your point. At the end, you still do not have your thesis – are you in agree with others or are you is disagree with others. However even, you put your thesis sentence at the end, this is still not good, as the audience has to wait for a long time to get the point from you.

It is very bad to miss the thesis sentence.

Understand the question before attempting to answer it, and make your question clear when you are asking questions.

If you don’t understand other’s questions, you may ask others to clarify their questions instead of guessing or making assumptions and give a vague answer or an answer not to the question. The same is when you are asking a question, try to make your question clear.

Be logical

Particularly when you are having a professional presentation or discussion with others like your supervisor, so that the audience can fully understand you and your work will have a solid deference against questions. Being logical can also benefit you in your research.

Here is a short conversation, and it may be able to take as an example for logic.

A. Why do not you eat in that room?
B. There is someone sleeping there.

Here B ‘s answer may be acceptable, but not logic. He missed some pieces when he is answering the question. If A is a very logical guy, he will further ask

A. Why you don’t want to eat in that room where there is someone sleeping there?
B. Because I don’t feel comfortable when I eat before a sleeping person.

When B gives the second answer, he gives A the missing piece and makes his answer more logic.

Being logical may help you in the research. Suppose you are A, when you see B having lunch in the Lab. You asked him, why don’t you eat in dining room? B answered, there is someone sleeping there. Most of you may say “Ok”, and then the conversation ends here. If you are a logical person, you might think is it because B don’t want to wake the sleeping guy as B may make lots of noise when he eat, or because B will not eat comfortably when there is someone sleeping around him, or because the one sleeping there is a very beautiful girl/cute boy…… So you ask B why B don’t want to eat in that room with a sleeping person? Then B gives you his answer “I don’t feel …..”. So you get some more information. If you keep ask B “why a sleeping person will make your eating uncomfortable”, you might be able to get more information about B.

Suppose now B is the nature or the object you are going to research. B cannot talk but it can give you answer in some other ways like experiments or simulations. The logic guy can keep asking the question and dig out the missing pieces by experiment or simulation or other methods in order to form a complete logical chain. By doing this he get more and more hidden information of the nature and this is the goal of a researcher. The guy with less or without logical sense may not be able to or don’t want to do so.

Don’t be “black box”.

Contact your supervisor if you have questions or problems in research which you cannot answer or you are not sure, your supervisor will be glad to answer your questions or have discussions with you in these situations. Sometime a question won’t cost your supervisor much time but can save you from struggling there for a long time, and your supervisor can also know what you are doing there. Even your supervisor may don’t know the answer to some of your questions, they would discuss with you and find a way to answer the question together.

Sometime you can consider your supervisor as your friend.
